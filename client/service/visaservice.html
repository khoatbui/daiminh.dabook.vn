1. Visa Exemption Program and its conditions

Currently the Government of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam is applying the Visa Exemption Program for citizens of 22 countries, including: Republic of Korea, Japan, Norway, Finland, Denmark, Sweden, Russia, Belorussia, UK, France, Germany, Spain, Italia; and the citizens of 9 countries in ASEAN: Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand.

With following conditions:

  • The citizens of Brunei will get the Visa Exemption limited to 14 days visit.
  • The citizens of the Philippines will get the Visa Exemption limited to 21 days visit.
  • The citizens of Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, Cambodia, and Laos will get the Visa Exemption limited to 30 days visit.
  • The citizens of Japan, Republic of Korea, Germany, France, UK, Italia, Spain, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Russia, and Finland will get the Visa Exemption limited to 15 days visit. In case the citizens need to stay longer than the limited time, they must apply for Visa valid 1 or 3 months for single entry or multiple entries.
  • The citizens of France or Chile who carry diplomatic passports or official passports will be able to stay in Vietnam for one continuous stay, or multiple entries which total length is not exceed 90 days and limited to 6 months in a row.
  • Citizens from the APEC members, who carry ABTC â€“ APEC Businessperson Transportation Card will have Visa Exemption limited to 60 days stay.
  • Visitors who enter Vietnam at any International Border Gate then travel to Phu Quoc Island will get the Visa Exemption for this stay limited to 15 days. Your passport needs to have at least 45 valid days left. After staying in Phu Quoc Island, the visitors want to travel to other part of Vietnam will need to apply for Visa (if require), this procedure will take place at the Vietnam Custom Office in Phu Quoc Island.

Notes: Passport needs to have validity of 06 months at lease. And each time of Visa Exemption must be 30 days apart.
